Q2 2026 — Key themes
Iran war ripple effects dominate Q2
Persistent Middle East security risks and energy market volatility ripple globally via higher costs, disrupted trade flows, and intensifying domestic political divisions over US/Israel/Iran roles.
Tariff chaos after SCOTUS ruling
Trump pursuing alternative tariff mechanisms after Supreme Court struck down IEEPA-based tariffs. Key partners delaying trade deal implementation until new US strategy becomes clear. Financial markets sensitive to AI equity correction and Fed independence risks.
Trump–Xi summit: tactical ease, no resolution
High-profile summit likely extends current US-China easing without resolving structural disputes over technology, market access and supply-chain security — leaving door open for later escalations.
Russia emboldened by higher oil prices
Moscow calculates it can still improve battlefield position before engaging seriously with ceasefire terms. Ukraine peace prospects remain limited through Q2.
Key elections: Hungary (Apr), Colombia (May–Jun), Armenia (Jun)
Hungary could end 15 years of euroskeptic governance. Colombia tests Latin America's rightward wave. Armenia's result directly impacts the Azerbaijani peace deal viability.
Gulf states on damage control
Emergency measures to restore investor confidence and revive tourism following reputational damage from the Iran war. Gulf positioning as stable commercial hubs under pressure.
Annual forecast — 8 key themes
US tariff policy volatile & legally challenged
SCOTUS likely to strike down IEEPA-based tariffs in 2026. White House will seek replacements using longer investigative tools, focusing on EU, Canada, Mexico. Tariffs will remain a coercive tactic regardless of ruling, undermining investor confidence and US manufacturing competitiveness.
Rare earth & memory chip supply chains at risk
US-China trade truce on semiconductors/rare earths is fragile. A breakdown would hit automotive and aerospace hardest. Surge in AI-driven demand for high-performance memory chips risks another semiconductor shortage akin to post-COVID disruptions.
AI bubble: rising risk, full burst unlikely
Valuations elevated with little near-term profitability. A burst would cascade through AI companies first, then data center/semiconductor suppliers. However, unlike the dotcom bubble, AI is not credit-driven — cash-rich tech companies reduce systemic risk. China closes gap with US frontier models in 2026, raising Western competitiveness concerns.
US military escalation in Western Hemisphere
Sustained airstrikes against drug trafficking targets in Venezuela likely. Ground invasion not expected — Maduro likely remains in power. US will also target cartels in Mexico/Colombia, risking bilateral tensions. Right-wing Colombia government (post-Aug) would increase military cooperation.
Israel: attrition and calculated escalation ahead of elections
Netanyahu alternates between attrition (Gaza, Lebanon) and escalation against Iran to secure another term. Strategy risks restarting major wars. US support continues even as pro-Israel sentiment weakens ahead of US midterms. Houthi attacks in Red Sea could restart.
US-China truce holds, but fragile
Interim deal holds through at least Trump's April China visit. Monthly escalation/de-escalation cycles likely around new tech restrictions and rare earth export controls. Post-midterms, Trump more willing to pursue comprehensive deal — but Chinese retaliation and congressional pushback will impede it. Taiwan blockade or invasion remains unlikely.
EU rearmament & strategic autonomy accelerate
EU reduces Russian energy and Chinese mineral dependence. Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism enters operational phase. SAFE defense loans begin disbursements. EU supports Ukraine unilaterally if US withdraws — sustaining elevated Russian hybrid threats.
Ukraine: negotiated settlement increasingly possible
Russia and Ukraine resist early concessions, but pressure accumulates. If Kremlin sees Trump-era settlement as optimal window before a hostile post-midterm Congress, a deal becomes possible. Any settlement will likely involve significant Ukrainian territorial losses and weak security guarantees.
